I am a postdoctoral researcher at [Columbia University Irving Medical Center](https://www.cuimc.columbia.edu/) in New York. My research sits at the intersection of **synthetic biology**, **generative AI**, and **regulatory genomics** — I build deep learning models that learn the encoding syntax of the genome and use them to design functional DNA/RNA elements from scratch.

During my PhD, I developed an AI-driven generative framework for **de novo promoter sequence design**, achieving high success rates in *in vivo* experimental validation. This work demonstrated how deep learning can navigate the vast sequence space of synthetic biology and produce regulatory elements with predictable, programmable behavior.

I am broadly interested in how the sequence and chemical properties of nucleic acid elements drive functional changes in gene regulation and disease. My long-term goal is to bridge **computational design** and **experimental validation** to accelerate the discovery of synthetic biology parts, with applications ranging from genetic circuits to next-generation therapeutic candidates.
